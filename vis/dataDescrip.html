Political Advertisements in the 2015-2016 Election Cycle

A Project by Tom Lucy and Duncan Gans, Fall 2017

The dataset we are using for this project was obtained from Government & Legal Studies Professor Michael Franz. The initial dataset contained information on all political ads, at both the state and federal levels, in the years 2015-2016. For our project, we will be focusing on solely US Senate, US House, and Presidential races - excluding ads for justice and gubernatorial races. In this subset of the data, there are about 303298 examples and 23 attributes.

Of the attributes that we are including, 6 are real values and the other 17 are catergorical. Rather than encoding categorical attributes, we are aggregating the data in the ways that we want to use it. Since our questions and visualizationts care more about trends in ads across time, we are much more interested in how many ads were run for US Senate races than the specifics of any particular US Senate ad.

These are the attributes in the dataset:

  1. L: Length of the political ad in seconds [real values]
  2. MARKET: The media market of the country in which the ad was run (e.g. Houston, Salt Lake, etc) [categorical]
  3. DISTRICT: The political district in which the race is occuring (e.g Mississippi 1st) [categorical]
  4. MEDIA: Type of media on which the ad was run (e.g. Spot TV) [categorical]
  5. AIRDATE: Date that the ad was run (1/1/15 - 12/31/16) [real values]
  6. AIRTIME: Time of day that the ad was run (00:00:00 - 23:59:59) [real values]
  7. DAY: General time of day that the ad was run, based off the time [categorical]
  8. PART: Genre of the channel on which the ad was run [categorical]
  9. STATION: Channel on which the ad was run [categorical]
  10. AFFILIATE: Large, national station with which the station that ran the ad is affiliated [categorical]
  11. PROGRAM: Name of the program during which that ad was run [categorical]
  12. PROGRAMTYPE: Genre of the program during which the ad was run [categorical]
  13. EST. COST: Estimated cost to run the ad (not to create the ad) [real values]
  14. ADTYPE: General type of ad (e.g. election, referendum, etc) [categorical]
  15. AFFILIATION: Party affiliated with the creator/sponsor of the ad [categorical]
  16. ELECTION: Election type of the ad that is being run (e.g. General) [categorical]
  17. ISSUE: Issues on which the ad is focused (e.g. Veterans, Taxes, etc) [categorical]
  18. LEVEL: Level of the race for which the ad was made (e.g. local, national, etc) [categorical]
  19. RACE: Race that the ad was for (e.g. US House, US Senate, etc) [categorical]
  20. TONE: General tone of the ad (e.g. Positive, Contrast, Negative) [categorical]
  21. YEAR: Year in which the ad was made [real values]
  22. NIELSENMAID: Number of the nielsen area in which the ad was run [real values]
  23. MARKET-STATE: A more general categorization of the market in which the ad was run, by state [categorical]
